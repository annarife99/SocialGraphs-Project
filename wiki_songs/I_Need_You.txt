The song was performed in their second film, ''[[Help! (film)|Help!]]''.\n\n==Composition and recording==\n\nThe song, simple in arrangement, was recorded on 15 and 16 February 1965.<ref name=\"lewisohn1p54\">{{cite book |first=Mark |last=Lewisohn |authorlink=Mark Lewisohn |title=The Beatles Recording Sessions |year=1988 |publisher=Harmony Books |location=New York |isbn=0-517-57066-1 |page=54}}</ref> It is commonly thought to address Harrison's relationship with [[Pattie Boyd]], whom he had met in March 1964 while filming ''[[A Hard Day's Night (film)|A Hard Day's Night]]''.<ref name=\"macdonald2p145-146\"/> (They married in January 1966.)<ref name=\"harryp199\">{{cite book |first=Bill |last=Harry |authorlink=Bill Harry |title=The Beatles Encyclopedia: Revised and Updated |year=2000 |publisher=Virgin Publishing |location=London |isbn=0-7535-0481-2 |page=199}}</ref>\n\nThe recording's distinctive lead guitar cadences, achieved by Harrison's first recorded use of a volume pedal,<ref name=\"macdonald2p145-146\"/><ref name=\"lewisohn1p54\"/> are based on common guitar suspended chords in the key of A, forming the introduction and most of the verse of the song and giving a quasi-modal effect that is relieved in the verse by a line in the relative minor (with a simple backing harmony from Lennon and McCartney), the whole making a fourteen-bar ternary verse-structure. This, after a repeat, segues easily into a second, bridge melody that introduces a cowbell for contrast and is based on a simple IV-V-I chord progression that passes through the dominant key to resolve back on the verse.<ref>http://www.icce.rug.nl/~soundscapes/DATABASES/AWP/iny.shtml Alan W.
